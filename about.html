From the warehouse floor to the dashboard.

How a decade of hands-on operations across the UAE and GCC grew into a discipline of reporting, analytics, and automation.

§ 01 — Biography

The work behind the numbers.

I did not start in analytics. I started in the store — receiving material, counting stock, and learning, line by line, how a construction operation actually moves.

That ground-floor view turned out to be the most valuable thing I own. When I later built ERP reports and dashboards, I already knew what the numbers meant on site — where variance hides, why a reconciliation breaks, and which figure a project manager actually needs at 7am.

Over more than ten years with ADC Energy Systems and Gulf Technical Construction Company in Dubai, I moved from inventory operations into reporting and analytics — automating manual workflows with Excel and Power Query, consolidating fragmented project data into a single source of truth, and building the KPI dashboards that management relies on. The throughline has never changed: accurate data, disciplined process, operational visibility.

§ 02 — Values

Principles that hold under audit.

Accuracy first

A report is only as good as the reconciliation behind it. Validate, then publish.

Automate the repeatable

If a person does it the same way twice a week, it should be a pipeline, not a chore.

Single source of truth

Fragmented spreadsheets create fragmented decisions. Consolidate relentlessly.

Clarity for decisions

A dashboard exists to make a choice obvious — not to look impressive.
